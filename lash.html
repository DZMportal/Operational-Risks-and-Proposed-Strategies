OPERATIONAL RISKS

RISKS ENCOUNTERED DURING LOADING-UNLOADING OPERATIONS

LASHING FAILURES

Lashing plays a crucial role in ensuring the stability of cargo and minimizing damage during transportation. Failure in the lashing system can lead to serious hazards, including damage to cargo, accidents, and significant financial losses. As noted in Cargo Securing Systems (n.d.), safety is a primary concern in shipping, and lashing is a fundamental component of this safety framework. Specifically, lashing involves securing containers to the vessel to prevent movement throughout transit. Inadequate lashing can cause containers to shift or fall overboard, resulting not only in cargo loss but also damage to the ship itself. In severe cases, such failures contribute to maritime accidents. Beyond safety, the efficiency of shipping operations heavily depends on effective lashing. A well-designed container lashing plan facilitates faster loading and unloading processes, while reducing the risk of accidents and cargo loss that could otherwise delay schedules.

Historical incidents illustrate the critical importance of proper lashing. For example, in 2013, the container ship MOL Comfort split in two in the Indian Ocean. Investigations revealed that the lashing system failed to withstand the forces generated by high waves, and the ship’s hull was compromised. Similarly, in 2018, the vessel YM Efficiency lost 81 containers off the coast of Australia. The investigation concluded that the lashing arrangements were inadequate for the weather conditions encountered. These cases clearly demonstrate that effective lashing is essential for maintaining the safety and structural integrity of container ships.

Human error is a predominant cause of lashing failure. Wilk et al. (2020) emphasize that crew negligence, improper fastening techniques, and insufficient training significantly contribute to lashing-related incidents. Such risks are further exacerbated by factors like fatigue and poor communication during cargo handling operations. Damage related to lashing commonly occurs when the equipment used is in poor condition and unable to securely fasten containers to the ship’s structure. The risk also increases when containers are fully loaded, underscoring the importance of careful planning for container storage to reduce potential damage (Twiller et al., 2024). Consequently, a properly developed lashing plan not only expedites cargo handling but also minimizes the likelihood of accidents and delays.

Poorly secured cargo can result in containers falling overboard, destabilizing the vessel, and causing severe or even fatal maritime incidents. The Australian Transport Safety Bureau (ATSB, 2020) detailed how insufficient lashing during loading and unloading contributed to the YM Efficiency incident. In June 2018, the ship lost containers following a sudden roll in heavy seas. The ATSB report highlighted that the stowage plan did not comply with the Cargo Securing Manual, leading to excessive strain on lashings and container stacks. This underscores the importance of utilizing computerized loading systems and ensuring that shore planners and ship officers receive adequate training.
